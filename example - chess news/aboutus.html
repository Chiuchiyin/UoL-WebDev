Small Beginnings

Our Roots at Stanford University

Chess News was not once a popular and internationally renowned brand. The company has its roots as a small startup that originated at Stanford University. The president of the Stanford chess club and an avid chess player came together to create what is now known as Chess News. It was the passion, curiosity, and desire to unite chess players across the world and provide a safe and pleasant environment to access up to date and accurate news.

Stanford University

Home to the Stanford Chess Club, which used to operate inside Tresidder Union, and welcomed students of all experience levels to participate.

Team Diversity

One of our company's core philosophies is the incorporation of diverse perspectives, which comes from the unique vantage points presented by our developers from all walks of life.

Our Team

And our Fundamental Values

To sustain the entire ecosystem of eager chess fans, we require a team of over 300+ employees from over 40 countries. Many are employed as remote developers and content editors. The decision to drop out of Stanford to pursue this avenue was a tough one. However, it was one of the best choices that could have been made. As a company, we promote kindness, generosity, and a joy of learning. We hope that our community can recognize and uphold these standards.

FAQ

Our Answers to our Most Popular Questions

Of course!

At Chess News, we firmly believe that it is never too late to pick up chess as a hobby. If you study chess books regularly and frequently play in longer time control games, it is feasible to become an expert level player within a few years. Despite this, it is true that chess is a sport that is by and large dominated by young players. If you wish to play chess at a professional level (i.e. making a living out of chess), it is recommended that you start at a much younger age, since your ability to learn new skills and process positional themes and patterns tends to be much sharper at a younger age.

Short answer: there is no 'best opening'!

And now, for the more lengthy answer... There are many popular openings to choose from, which ranges from the Italian game to the English opening, and the Queen's pawn. Choosing an opening depends on several factors such as your experience level, the level of aggressiveness of your play, how tactically or positionally sound your playstyle is, and how willing you are to learn many intricate variations. With many factors to take into consideration, there is no definitive way of declaring a 'best' opening.

Learn a few openings in detail

It is much more efficient for a beginner or intermediate level player to hone in on a few common openings such as the Sicilian Defense, the French Defense, and the Caro-Kann defence. This allows you to understand the general plans for white and black, enables you to be in control of the game (i.e. by playing in a theoretically optimal way), and presents you with a clear course of action that gives you a 'home field advantage' by being prepared for different possible lines. This approach is not limited to memorizing opening theory, but also serves to reinforces fundamental principles that constitute good chess.
