Top Players | FIDE

Last Updated November 31, 2021

Fabiano Caruana
FIDE Rating: 2791 | Rank No. 3
Caruana is a chess prodigy who became a Grandmaster at the age of 14 years and 11 months. He has reached a peak rating of 2844 in 2014 and is the youngest Grandmaster in the history of Italy and America.
Ding Liren
FIDE Rating: 2799 | Rank No. 2
Ding holds three Chinese Chess Champion titles and was the winner of the 2019 Grand Chess Tour. In 2019, Ding won the Sinquefield Cup and ended Carlsen's reign of consecutive playoff wins since 2007.
Magnus Carlsen
FIDE Rating: 2855 | Rank No. 1
In 2013, Carlsen defeated Viswanathan Anand and became World Chess Champion. Renowned for his endgame mastery, he has been compared to former world champions Karpov and Fischer.
Anish Giri
FIDE Rating: 2774 | Rank No. 6
Giri is a world class chess player that was awarded the Grandmaster title in 2009 at a meager age of 14 years, 7 months and 2 days. As of August of 2021, Giri is the number 1 top ranked player in the Netherlands.
Ian Nepomniachtchi
FIDE Rating: 2782 | Rank No. 5
Nepomniachtchi was victorious in the 2010 and 2020 Russian Superfinal and also won the 2010 European Individual titles. In 2016, he won the Tal Memorial in addition to the Aeroflot Open in 2008 and 2015.
Levon Aronian
FIDE Rating: 2772 | Rank No. 4
Aronian attained his peak FIDE rating of 2830 in March of 2014. In 2005, he was named the best sportsman of Armenia. In 2009, he was awarded the title of Honoured Master of Sport of the Republic of Armenia in 2009.
Alireza Firouzja
FIDE Rating: 2770 | Rank No. 9
Firouzja won the Iranian Chess Championship at age 12 and became a Grandmaster at age 14. At the 2019 World Rapid Chess Championship, Firouzja became the second youngest 2700-rated player to win a silver medal at the event.
Wesley So
FIDE Rating: 2772 | Rank No. 8
So is the current U.S. Chess Champion and held the title on three occassions: in 2017, 2020, and 2021. On March of 2017, he was ranked second in the world and had a FIDE rating of 2822 - the fifth-highest rating in history.
Alexander Grischuk
FIDE Rating: 2773 | Rank No. 7
Grischuk was the Russian champion in 2009 and is the three-time world blitz chess champion in 2006, 2012, and 2015. At the Chess Olympiads, Grischuk won two team gold-medals, three team silvers, one team bronze, and one individual bronze medal.
